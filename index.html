📋 Assumptions & limitations of this calculator tap to expand
How tax is treated in this calculator:
  • The tool calculates corpus using the portfolio's blended effective tax rate — a weighted average of how each instrument is taxed, based on your allocation.
  • Each instrument has an indicative effective tax rate baked in: MF SWP ~4.4% (only ~35% of redemption is gain, taxed at 12.5% LTCG), PPF 0% (fully exempt), FD/SCSS ~20% (interest taxable at slab rate, assumes 20% slab), Pension ~8% (partly taxable after commutation), Rental ~14% (after 30% std deduction at 20% slab).
  • For reference, the "Tax on withdrawal" card also shows the progressive slab tax — what you'd pay if all withdrawals were fully taxable (India new regime FY 2026-27 + 4% cess). This is shown only to help compare. The corpus calculation uses the portfolio rate, not the slab rate.
  • If your portfolio is fully in tax-efficient instruments (MF SWP, PPF), the calculator shows a lower corpus than a naive slab-based calculation would.
  • If your real income slab is higher than 20% (e.g. you have other taxable income), actual FD/SCSS tax burden will be higher than modelled.
Modelling assumptions:
  • Inflation is treated as a single constant rate for the full retirement period (in reality it varies year to year)
  • Investment returns are constant — no market volatility, no sequence-of-returns risk modelled
  • Tax slabs frozen at India FY 2026-27 new regime — slabs typically rise ~5-7% per year, not modelled
  • Effective tax rate per instrument is an indicative average (e.g. MF SWP ~4.4%, FD ~20% at an assumed 20% slab)
  • Monthly compounding used — return applied monthly, withdrawal taken monthly, inflation grows monthly
  • Corpus calculation assumes withdrawals start at retirement, balance ends at zero at year N
Not included in the model:
  • Healthcare inflation typically runs 8-9% in India — add 12-15% buffer to corpus for medical costs
  • No emergency reserve (3-6 months living expenses) modelled separately
  • Currency risk — assumes pure INR. NRIs need to plan repatriation timing & FX separately
  • No large one-time expenses (children's weddings, home purchase, travel) modelled
  • No estate / inheritance planning included
  • Surcharge on income above ₹50L not modelled (most retirees stay below this)
Instrument return assumptions (nominal, pre-tax, annual):
  • MF SWP equity/balanced: 8.5%  ·  Bank FD (senior citizen): 7.0%
  • Pension/annuity: 6.0% (auto-holder — balances total to 100%)
  • PPF/Debt MF: 7.2%  ·  Rental yield (net): 3.5%
  • SCSS: 8.2% (govt guaranteed, hard-capped at ₹30L per person)
Bottom line: This tool gives you a directionally accurate baseline. Build in a 15-20% buffer over the calculated corpus for the real-world variability above, and revisit your plan every 2-3 years as inputs change.
